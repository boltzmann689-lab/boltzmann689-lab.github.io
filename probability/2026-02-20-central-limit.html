Central Limit Theorem (CLT)

2026-02-20

The CLT says that properly normalized sums of i.i.d. random variables converge in distribution to a normal distribution. If $X_i$ have mean $\mu$ and variance $\sigma^2$:

$$\frac{\sum_{i=1}^n X_i - n\mu}{\sigma\sqrt{n}} \xrightarrow{d} \mathcal{N}(0,1).$$

This explains why Gaussian approximations are ubiquitous in statistics.
