Law of Large Numbers

2026-02-20

The Law of Large Numbers (LLN) states that the sample average converges to the expected value as sample size grows. For i.i.d. random variables $X_1,\dots,X_n$ with mean $\mu$:

$$\overline{X}_n = \frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^n X_i \xrightarrow{\;p\;} \mu.$$

This means for any $\epsilon>0$, $\Pr(|\overline{X}_n - \mu|>\epsilon)\to 0$ as $n\to\infty$.

Example: empirical averages of coin flips converge to $0.5$.
